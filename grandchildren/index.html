Building Legacies, Not Debt

When Barbara Mitchell's granddaughter was accepted to her dream college, the 68-year-old retired teacher felt both pride and panic. The tuition bill was $35,000 per year, and Barbara desperately wanted to help — but not at the expense of her own retirement security.

"I'd saved my whole life for retirement," Barbara explains from her sunny kitchen in Henderson, Nevada. "But seeing my granddaughter's face when she got that acceptance letter... I knew I had to find a way to help without depleting my nest egg."

Barbara's solution? A strategy that's quietly revolutionizing how grandparents support their grandchildren's education — without the financial sacrifice previous generations endured.

"I can give my grandchildren the gift of education while still protecting my own financial future. It's the best of both worlds."
